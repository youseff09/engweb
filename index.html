III. 2020s STRUCTURAL RACISM

Today, racism is not typically enforced by laws, but is woven into systems like healthcare, housing, and criminal justice. These systemic barriers work together to maintain inequities.

For example, neighborhoods with more Black residents often experience heavier police presence and fewer economic opportunities. These barriers function in ways that produce outcomes of disadvantage similar to the 1920s.
