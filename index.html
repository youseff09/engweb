1920s VS 2020s

BY SALMA, YUAN, YOUSSEF, MARYAM

I. 1920s RACISM

In the 1920s, racism was a normal and accepted part of American life. Many people believed white Americans were better than others, and this belief was supported by the government and society. Jim Crow laws forced Black Americans to live separately and gave them fewer rights, especially in the South. Racism was not hiddenâ€”it was open and legal.

Groups like the Ku Klux Klan became very powerful and spread racist ideas across the country. They used fear and violence to push their beliefs that America should only belong to white Protestants.

II. 2020s SYSTEMIC

Today, racism looks different. Laws no longer allow segregation, but inequality still exists in less obvious ways. This is called systemic racism. It means that unfair treatment is built into systems like housing, schools, and jobs, even if no one says racist things out loud.

For example, neighborhoods treated unfairly in the past often still have worse schools today. Because of this, race still affects how easy or hard it is for someone to move up in life.

III. THE DREAM

The American Dream is the idea that anyone can succeed if they work hard enough. In the 1920s, this mostly applied to white Americans. In the 2020s, the Dream is more inclusive in theory, but not in reality. Some people start life with more advantages than others. It can feel like some are running a race with no obstacles, while others have many barriers in their way.

IV. GATSBY CONNECTION

Tom Buchanan represents the racist beliefs of the 1920s. He believes white people should stay in control and is scared of losing his power. Even though Gatsby is rich, he is still looked down on, proving that social status mattered more than hard work alone. This connects to today because power is still often protected by those who already have it.
